Site Delivery Workflow

Sitecore Experience Accelerator is not just an extension of the Sitecore interface, but a new way of approaching the production of websites. By enabling parallel work streams (content, creative, UX, coding,) you can not only speed up the production of websites but improve the quality of the work to allow the work streams to feedback and validate each other's work. This avoids the classic situation of having the entire site built before entering any content, only to find the templates are not able to service the needs of the authors.

By allowing the authors to start entering real content into a wireframe environment, built on Sitecore with SXA, your UI and creatives can be working with real content, and the project board can be constantly validating the work. For those interested in Content Strategy, this might be the tool you have been looking for to facilitate your approach without boxing you into a particular way of working.

Time To Market

In a traditional project, the Site Production is carried out in a linear fashion, with the user experience and design work done before coding begins. Content Entry is left until all these things have been finished. Even if these streams of work are successful, the time to market can be slow.

With SXA, these streams can be brought into a parallel Site Production (as the diagram below indicates). This way, once a basic user experience plan is in place we can get started on the platform straight away.


Wireframe your site... while delivering your site!

You no longer need to go outside the CMS to deliver your wireframes. Using our wireframe theme, you can design these directly within the site you are working on. As a UX designer, you can be editing the page layout in such a way that will not get thrown out the moment you're done with it, but rather, is immediately usable to your content authors. So you can be fine tuning your header while at the same time your content authors are already adding articles to the site - because you've already designed that part. This is because the presentation is separated from content and managed on its own, using Partial Designs where the UX users can work independently from other team members.

While your editors...

For a great deal of content, you can begin the process of authoring before you even have any layouts produced. In Sitecore, much of the core content of the site (such as articles or products etc.) will be produced using an Article template. Authors can be entering the core article content via the Sitecore Content Editor or a temporary wireframe layout in the Experience Editor. As long as they have access to the core fields (such as main, the body-text and images), these can be placed or moved later by the UX designer using SXA.

This is possible because layouts for core content are managed using Partial Designs. So while the content editors are working, this partial design can be altered without affecting their work. Or, it can be completely replaced with a new one later, by simply switching to a different partial in a Design.

For large operations, put some thought into the experience of your authors as the website is developing. We can provide consultancy on how best to use SXA in parallel production scenarios - there are many different way that SXA can facilitate your needs.

At the same time your Front End developers....

There is a whole set of good news for your front-end developers. One of the best things is that they don't really need to change their tools and they don't need to know much about SXA or Sitecore. Because of how SXA operates, they don't even need to build HTML for the website. All they need to do is export the site using Creative Exchange and they get everything authors provided to them in a convenient Zip file. Once they unpack it on their machine, they can crack the files open in their favourite Sublime Texts or WebStorms and edit those as they would normally do. They can add any missing styling to the pages in places which are clearly highlighted in the markup, put additional files in Media Library and supplement themes with any styling changes your branding might require. Once that's done, they can Zip the site again and upload it back using Creative Exchange. SXA will take care of the rest - it will add the classes to the components, put the files where they belong in the Media Library and even make the additional classes re-usable to components on other pages so you can re-use those later with new content.

Another good news is that CSS and JavaScript files in themes are logically divided into chunks that deal with one component at a time. Don't worry about performance though - after everything is done and deployed our Asset Optimizer will concatenate and zip the files so your work always performs at its best!

Meanwhile at the Back End...

While we aim at solving most of your common needs, there still will be work for your developers. You need your Data Templates setup (don't worry if you don't know what it means - your developers do), you need your site deployed and maybe caches tweaked.

Are there any integrations with your back-end systems, or is it missing any components? No worries - they can do it all in the peace and quiet of their basement, while your site layout is being put together by the designers and your message is being delivered by the editors...

SXA comes with documentation and best practices for your developers, so that all they do is of the same excellent quality as the platform they are building upon.
